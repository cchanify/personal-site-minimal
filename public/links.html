Links
Things I've been reading and found useful. Most are security-related since that's my day job, but there's other stuff mixed in too.
Security & Privacy
Practical guides and deep technical analysis on security topics.
  • The WIRED Guide to Protecting Yourself From Government Surveillance
    WIRED, November 2024
    Comprehensive guide to digital privacy tools, secure communications, and operational security. Covers VPNs, encrypted messaging, device security, and threat modeling frameworks.
  • An in-depth guide to choosing a VPN
    David Huerta, June 2018
    Technical analysis of VPN selection criteria including logging policies, encryption standards, and jurisdiction considerations. Explains VPN limitations and threat modeling approaches.
  • AI for Security: Eight Areas of Opportunity
    Menlo Ventures
    Investment perspective on AI applications in cybersecurity. Covers threat detection, incident response automation, and market opportunities in security AI.
  • Why did we need to build our own SIEM?
    Rippling Engineering
    Technical case study on building custom security information and event management systems. Discusses limitations of existing SIEM solutions and design decisions for scale.
  • Key Kubernetes audit logs for monitoring cluster security
    Datadog Security Team
    Practical guide to Kubernetes security monitoring through audit logs. Covers specific log events to monitor, detection strategies for common attack patterns, and implementation examples.
Technology & Industry
Broader thinking about technology trends and industry direction.
  • Big Data is Dead
    Jordan Tigani, February 2023
    Analysis of the evolution from "big data" to modern data architectures. Argues that most data problems don't require big data solutions and examines current data engineering practices.
  • It's Time to Build
    Marc Andreessen, April 2020
    Call for increased focus on building physical and digital infrastructure. Written during COVID-19, advocates for American technological and industrial capacity building.
  • The Techno-Optimist Manifesto
    Marc Andreessen, October 2023
    Manifesto advocating for accelerated technological progress and innovation. Argues for removing regulatory barriers and embracing technological solutions to global challenges.
  • My techno-optimism
    Vitalik Buterin, November 2023
    Ethereum founder's perspective on technology's role in solving global problems. Discusses decentralization, AI safety, and the balance between technological progress and risk mitigation.
  • Approaches to Software Engineering that Worked For Me
    John Loeber, August 2023
    Personal reflections on effective software engineering practices. Covers career development, technical decision-making, and lessons learned from industry experience.
  • Overview & Applications of Large Language Models
    Leigh Marie Braswell
    Overview of LLM capabilities and applications across industries with focus on security implications. Covers both defensive applications like threat detection and offensive concerns including automated social engineering.
  • An Opinionated Guide to ML Research
    John Schulman
    Practical advice for conducting machine learning research. Covers experimental methodology, paper writing, and career development in ML research.
  • How To Do Great Work
    Paul Graham
    Essay on the principles and practices of exceptional work across different fields. Covers motivation, focus, originality, and the iterative process of creating significant contributions.
Research & Papers
Academic and industry research that's shaped my thinking.
  • Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System
    Satoshi Nakamoto, October 2008
    Original Bitcoin whitepaper introducing blockchain technology and decentralized consensus mechanisms. Describes proof-of-work systems, transaction verification, and network security incentives.
  • Using Static Analysis to Find Bugs in the Real World
    Engler et al., Stanford
    Research on using static analysis tools to find security vulnerabilities in production code. Covers techniques for reducing false positives and integrating automated analysis into development workflows.
  • The Malicious Use of Artificial Intelligence
    EFF, February 2018
    Comprehensive analysis of AI security risks including automated hacking, surveillance, and disinformation. Examines both current threats and future risks from adversarial AI applications.
  • DEF CON 26 Voting Village Report
    DEF CON, 2018
    Technical analysis of vulnerabilities found in electronic voting systems during DEF CON 26. Documents security flaws and provides recommendations for improving election infrastructure security.
  • MIT Pathway to Fusion Energy
    Zach Hartwig, MIT 2017
    Technical overview of fusion energy research and development pathways. Covers physics principles, engineering challenges, and timeline for practical fusion power generation.
